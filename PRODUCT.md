# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Engineering college students in India — first through fourth year, organized into departments (CS, ECE, ME, CE, IS, Design, Bio, Econ), sections (A/B/C), and year cohorts. Access is gated: students verify with a USN (university serial number) and an ID upload; an admin approves them before they can participate. Sessions happen in short bursts — between lectures, in the canteen, from a hostel room. The primary jobs: asking peers for academic help without embarrassment, staying aware of what's happening on campus (events, projects, who's in their department), and building the light social fabric of college life.

## Product Purpose

Z3NTRUM is a closed, verified college social platform. The defining mechanic is cooperative: students post help requests, peers offer help via DM, and the conversation moves into messages. Beyond help, there's a general feed for updates and projects, group chats organized by section/department/year/college, campus events with RSVP, and peer discovery by name, USN, or department. It is not a public network, a résumé tool, or a coursework submission system — it is the digital version of the quad: a familiar, trusted space where students from the same institution find each other.

## Brand Personality

Warm, collegiate, cooperative. The tone is unhurried and human — more like a bulletin board in a department hallway than a social media feed. Not aspirational-corporate. Not gaming-community loud. Confident enough to be itself.

## Anti-references

- **LinkedIn** — corporate polish, résumé anxiety, cold professional distance, connection-count metrics
- **Discord** — dark-by-default gaming aesthetic, channel clutter, anonymous handles over real names
- **Canvas / Blackboard / any LMS** — institutional grey, bureaucratic, transactional, zero personality
- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — metric-hero templates, cold blues, enterprise spacing, widget grids
- **BeReal / TikTok** — high-stimulation, algorithmically manipulated, built on anxiety

## Design Principles

1. **Cohort identity is the social fabric.** Department, section, and year are how students understand their place in the institution. The UI should surface these structures proudly — not flatten everyone into an undifferentiated feed.
2. **Help as a first-class action.** The platform's most distinctive mechanic is peer help: ask → offer → DM. Every surface should make this feel low-friction and socially safe — asking for help should never feel like a confession.
3. **Vetted = intimate.** The approval flow and USN verification mean users know each other, or could. Design for that trust: real names, faces, and cohort context over anonymous handles and public-internet caution.
4. **Warm, not clinical.** An education product does not need to feel sterile. The warm off-white palette, TheTide display face, and department color pills already say this clearly — follow their lead.
5. **Short-session clarity.** Students use this between lectures, not in focused 2-hour work sessions. Prioritize legibility at a glance, quick actions, and an uncluttered feed over power-user density.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Department color pills must not rely on color alone to convey identity (label text required alongside color — already satisfied). Support both light and dark themes with full contrast compliance in each. Reduced motion: no autoplay animations on the main feed.
